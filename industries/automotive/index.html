Georgia automotive & EV

From Kia opening the state's first auto assembly plant in 2009 to Hyundai's $7.6B Metaplant rolling out IONIQs in 2024, Georgia has become America's most concentrated EV-manufacturing buildout. Production, employment, plant-by-plant scorecard, and the milestones behind the corridor.

Heads-up:

The new Detroit of the South.

Since 2018, Hyundai Motor Group, SK On, LG Energy Solution, and Rivian have collectively committed more than $28B to vehicle and battery plants in Georgia — the largest cluster of EV-supply-chain investment in any U.S. state. Kia's West Point assembly plant, the original anchor since 2009, makes the Telluride, Sorento, and K5.

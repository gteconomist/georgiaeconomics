Recent Performance

Savannah's economy is outperforming both Georgia and the US through the first half of 2026. Payroll employment is growing at a 2.4% annualized clip — well above the 0.5% national pace — with the strongest gains in transportation & warehousing, durable-goods manufacturing, and leisure & hospitality. The unemployment rate at 3.1% has held a full point below the US average for three consecutive quarters, and the labor force has expanded as in-migration continues to feed the metro.

Container volume at the Port of Savannah rebounded sharply in late 2025 after the early-2025 tariff-driven inventory pull-forward unwound. Through April 2026, the Georgia Ports Authority moved 2.4M TEUs, on track for a record year, supported by the new Ocean Terminal redevelopment coming online in stages.

Manufacturing & the EV Cluster

The Hyundai Metaplant America in Bryan County reached full single-shift production in late 2025 and is ramping to a 300,000-vehicle annual run rate. Suppliers continue to cluster: 13 announced facilities now total over $3B in committed investment and 6,500 projected jobs. Federal EV-credit policy remains the key swing factor for the medium-term trajectory.

Logistics & Trade

Transportation & warehousing employment has expanded 18% since 2020, more than triple the US rate. The completion of Mason Mega Rail has reduced inland-port turnaround times, strengthening Savannah's competitive position relative to Charleston and Norfolk for Southeast cargo. Tariff policy remains the dominant downside risk: every 10-point rise in the average effective tariff has historically reduced GPA throughput by roughly 4–6% over the following two quarters.

Housing

House prices continue to appreciate at a moderate 3.6% YoY pace — slower than the 2021–2022 boom but still above the national average. Supply has been responsive: single-family permits ran at 11.2 per 1,000 residents in 2025, the highest of any GA MSA. Affordability has nonetheless deteriorated; the median home now costs roughly 4.7× median household income, vs. 3.4× in 2019.

Outlook

Baseline forecasts have Savannah outpacing Georgia and the US through 2029 on employment, GMP, and population. The metro's heavy goods-movement and manufacturing tilt makes it more cyclically exposed than the typical US metro, but its structural drivers — port capacity, the EV cluster, and net domestic migration into the Sun Belt — remain intact. Long-term risk exposure ranks 168th of 411 US metros (2nd quintile, modeled proxy).
